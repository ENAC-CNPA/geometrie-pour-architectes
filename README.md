# Geometry for Architects

## About
geometrie-pour-architectes is developed as a research project at the CNPA lab of EPFL and the MHA lab of ENSAG.

It is a web viewer built on Speckle, a collaborative platform for the AEC sector:
https://github.com/specklesystems 

This viewer is developed to disseminate geometric figures related to the history of construction and the sciences (perspective, stereotomy, gnomonics). This includes the study of treatises or of architectural systems such as vaults.
The digital redrawing of these historical sources is enriched with 3D interpretation and sequential display of the geometric operations.

## Current corpus
The platform is currently being developed using a limited corpus.
The models are designed using the TopSolid CAD/CAM parametric software. This method was developed by Professor Bernard Cache as part of the “Geometry for Architects” course at EPFL.
The purpose of the web viewer is to make this content available to a wider audience through an open-access platform.

The viewer benefits from the parallel development of a Speckle connector for the TopSolid software:
https://github.com/ENAC-CNPA/speckle-sharp
